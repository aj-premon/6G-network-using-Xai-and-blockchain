The 6G Security Paradox

. Future 6G networks will connect billions of devices at near-instant speeds, generating an
enormous amount of data.

. Traditional centralized security models are too slow for this environment, creating network
bottlenecks and risking the leakage of private user information.


The Three-Pillar Solution

. Federated Learning (The Privacy Guard): Instead of sending raw data to a central
server, Al models are trained locally directly on user devices to keep private data secure
and reduce latency.

· Blockchain (The Digital Notary): A lightweight, tamper-proof digital ledger is utilized to
ensure trust and prevent hackers from altering security records.

· Explainable Al (The Translator): It solves the Al "black box" problem by providing
transparent, understandable reasoning for every automated security alert and decision.

Expected Impact

. By integrating these three technologies, the project envisions a highly secure, privacy-
preserving 6G infrastructure.

. The framework is deliberately designed to be lightweight, preserving battery life on
resource-constrained devices like sensors while maintaining the speed needed for
massive network connectivity.

· Ultimately, the goal is to deploy a reliable, intelligent system that continuously self-learns
and adapts to neutralize brand-new cyber threats as they emerge in real-time.
